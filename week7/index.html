STEALTH WAR is a collection created by Adam Harvey that addresses “the rise of surveillance, the power of those who surveil, and the growing need to exert more control over privacy”. The collection builds upon his previous work, CV Dazzle to continue to explore how fashion can challenge surveillance. CV Dazzle is a form of camouflage from computer vision created in 2010. Stealth wear is inspired by traditional islamic dress and the notion that clothing separates man and god. The clothings uses silver-plated fabric that reflects thermal radiation, to avoid overhead thermal surveillance.

The MAGIC LEAP BRACELET is a design patented by Magic Leap. The bracelet would allow fast track access to apps when using an AR Headseat. Users would see a virtual layer on top of the bracelet charms when using a AR Headset. Each charm would represent a different app and users could access the app by touching the corresponding charm on the bracelet.

Modem, an Amsterdam based design studio and Studio Bjørn Ibsen, a Copenhagen based 3D design studio, collaborated to create an imagined visualisation of the Magic Leap Bracelet based on existing patent. They also did so for patents without public executions yet.

The ADIDAS GMR OR GAMER was a wearable technology that converted real-life personal football data into EA Sports FIFA Mobile, a football simulation game. The GMR technology worked through a chip inserted into the adidas shoe insole of person’s dominant foot. The technology measured attributes like speed, distance, shot power and passing – all of which are converted into the game. As user improve their physical performance data, they earn rewards to improve their ranking and team. However the exact data was not available to users.

SEMINAR

Physical computing refers to creating interactive systems that sense and respond to the world around them. It uses hardware and software to build systems that engage inputs and outputs. This encompasses cybernetics, the study of control and communication between natural and artificial systems.

“A framework for understanding human being’s relationship to the digital world”

Physical computing exists in many forms in our day to day lives:

  • Devices: phones, laptops, computers, headphones etc
  • Wearables: smart watch
  • Public transport: stop buttons, displays
  • Shopping: rfid, self checkouts, CCTV, ATMs
  • Homes: smart keys, fobs, appliances
