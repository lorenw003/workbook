HUNTNGATHER

CODEWORDS or Creative Coding and Typography is a studio taught at RMIT by Karen ann Donnachie and Andy Simionato. The studio asks students to ‘reread’ critical texts on art and language through the use of computer code to explore how reading and graphic design are transforming in the digital age. Students are taught to expand their design practice by combining code and typography.

This website is a showcase of student work from this course in 2024. I really like the way the tile “codewords” appears static with electricity and changes based on distance from the mouse. Likewise the student work collated on this page is each feature unique and interesting storytelling. Oscillate requires user to put on their webcam and then “oscillate” their body to move the reflected video and reveal hidden text.

HEAD BANGER is an experimental game by Hello Monday, featuring music from Dracaris’s album “Nowhere”. The game’s aesthetic is inspired by the album specifically the themes of metal rock, hallucination and schizophrenia. Within the game the player is shown as panel featuring their web camera footage on one side of board with blocks in the centre. A skull on the opposite side releases a fireball when the users opens their mouth. The player must then prevent the ball from going off the screen and make it hit the block by moving their head to move the panel and bounce the ball off. They can also head bang to make the ball bounce.

This experience was made using three.js and Javscript. The user consistently maintains enough agency to play the game. However the motions required itself are not very precise and would have limited use elsewhere. However the user does not maintain agency of their image as it not stated whether it is stored, just asked for permission.

WEBCAM is a series of painting created by Atshushi Kasao based on image from webcams found on the internet. Atshushi would approach Web camera owners for images and use original rendering software, SIC (Synergistic Image Creator) to create Webcam Art. Afterwards she would send the images to web cam owners for feedback and send them a poster-sized copy if requested.

Synergistic Image Creator is series of GIMP source plug-in modules. GIMP is is an open-source photo retouch software working on Linux and Windows. The images created from SIC include a history of the image process. So anyone can access the history to modify it and apply it to their own images. Web cam owners could find and use effects applied to their images on other images.

CHOSEN WORK: Slave to the Algorithm

Slave to the Algorithm is a studio taught at RMIT by Karen ann Donnachie and Andy Simionato. It focuses on emerging design practices in generative design, parametric typography, and data-visualisation. The subsequent works showcase different forms of generative art and data visualisation from different stimuli and parametric fonts.

  • Manual text inputs, which act as stimuli for generative art and parametric fonts
  • Web camera outputs that visualise the data points used and how they are used with coloured dots and graphs
  • Keystrokes (most commonly from the arrows keys) used to change images to look through a gallery of images..
  • Buttons used to submit information in an input or move to next stage of a website
  • Cursor trail used to show the cursor motion and for aesthetics
  • Auditory input used as input for generative art or parametric fonts or to change the colour, style, or state of a page
  • Links and clickable text used to change pages or trigger an element to move
  • Videos and background audio used for aesthetic effect or for data visualisation

SEMINAR

Computer Vision & the Black Mirror

Computer vision describes how computers can see and interpret images and videos, similar to humans. This includes the categorisation of images and facial identification. Facial recognition can be achieved with 15 basic points on person’s face. This information is stored in passports and government databases and used to identify people in CCTV and other footage.

USER INPUT

For this activity we were asked to consider different ways users can interact with web based script. Ways we thought included manual text input, keystrokes, mouse and touchpad gestures, clicks including double clicks, hovering, voice input, audio files, tapping physical tech, clicking hardware buttons (ie. volume button on Iphone), video input, live video.

We were also asked to consider alternative that currently don’t exist. There is potential to incorporate smell, which detects certain smell similar to how a smoke detector detects smoke.
