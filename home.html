FILE: WEEK 1

INSPIRATION: Mosiac Virus, Myriad, Glitch Textiles, Maria Szakats
SKILLS AQUIRED: Basic HTML
This was our first week and introduction to interactive media and WWW. We considered their meaning through how we interact with them - gestures - and their structure or anatomy. We set up VS code and got started exploring HTML.

FILE: WEEK 2

INSPIRATION: Spellverse, Movie Palace Online, 45 Denim Kurata
SKILLS AQUIRED: Paper and Modular Prototyping, Crazy Eights, Githhub
This week we considered how the web has evolved overtime resulting in many different design styles and layouts. We began our own designs starting ideation and learning how to design for the web physically - prototypes - and digitally.

FILE: WEEK 3

INSPIRATION: Parametro studios, Antione Wodniack, Agorespace
SKILLS AQUIRED: Responsive/Fluid design, Media queries, Variable Fonts, Custom Fonts, Audio, Video
This week focused on learning how to publish to the WWW specifically considering grid (layout), responsive design and typography. These considerations informed the personalisation of our workbooks including learning how to use new html tags and css. As well as personal research for our unique visions.

FILE: WEEK 4

INSPIRATION: Momument Valley, Bruno Simon, An Unknown ARG
SKILLS AQUIRED: Shapes and Lines in p5, Algorithmic Thinking
This week we focused on generative art forms - instructional and algorithmic - and started learning p5 as a medium of generative art. We continued to work on our workbooks and saw the Kusama exhibition at the NGV.

FILE: WEEK 5

INSPIRATION: Codewords, Head Banger, Webcam Art
SKILLS AQUIRED: Images and Live Video in p5, If Statements
This week we considered user input including facial recognition. We also considered the use and capacity of our data and what agency we maintain once it is given. We continued to learn p5 and update our workbooks.

FILE: WEEK 6

INSPIRATION: Film Flowers, Ji Park, Inclusive Sans
SKILLS AQUIRED: Prerecorded Audio, Captured Audio and Voice to Text in p5
This week we considered the physical forms of data within international data cables, data ranches and networks. Further, we discussed the physical effects of this data storage - environmental, privacy - and how they are commodified and presented to tell narratives. We continued to learn p5 and update our workbooks.
